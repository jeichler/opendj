Welcome to OpenDJ

Have you ever been at an event and disliked the music being played?
The idea of OpenDJ is to solve this problem by applying open source principles to the music playlist of an event.

How does it work?

Participants of an event can contribute to the playlist by adding tracks. Everybody can see and add to the playlist, no special accounts or apps needed, no access to local WiFi or special devices.

What are Curators?

Curators can edit the playlist (sorting/grouping by genre, deleting). Curators get more detailed information about a track (e.g. genre, bpm, danceability, likes/dislikes by users). There is no special skill needed to be curator, just a sense for music. Of course you could hire a professional DJ as additional curator if you like.

Where does the music come from?

The music is being played by a streaming provider, e.g. Spotify. As Event Owner, you need to provide your streaming provider credentials. OpenDJ is running in the internet and uses your streaming provider APIs to execute the playlist on a local device of the event owner.
